# GRAPHICS & RENDERING ANALYSIS (isopolis.html)

## SUMMARY (current visual identity and technique)

Isopolis is a **flat-plane isometric diorama** rendered with Three.js r128 (loaded from CDN, line 507). The visual identity is a "toy model / tabletop miniature" look: a single flat ground plane painted by a 2D canvas texture, populated with small, brightly vertex-colored, low-poly extruded boxes/cylinders/cones that pop up with a springy scale-Y animation. There is no elevation anywhere — the world is a strictly flat `PlaneGeometry` (line 666); "water" and "grass" are just pixel colors in the ground canvas, not geometry.

The style reads as "cute but limited" for concrete, identifiable reasons: (1) all 3D objects share **one `MeshLambertMaterial` with `flatShading:true` and vertex colors** (line 988) — no PBR, no roughness/metalness, no reflections, no texture maps on any building, so every surface is a flat matte facet; (2) the camera is a **locked 35.26° orthographic** rig with only 90°-snap rotation (lines 621–636, 3345–3346), giving the fixed, diagrammatic SimCity-2000 feel; (3) **tone mapping is deliberately disabled** (`NoToneMapping`, line 611) to keep colors "punchy," which also means no filmic/ACES rolloff, no bloom, no HDR; (4) day/night is a **binary toggle that lerps between two hardcoded color sets** (lines 4413–4431), not a continuous sun arc — the sun never actually moves. Windows, detail, and "life" (cars, smoke, clouds, city lights) are all faked cheaply with sprites, points, and tiny boxes. It's charming and coherent, but it's a diorama lit by a static three-point rig, not a world.

## MECHANICS (name — line range — technique details)

- **Renderer setup — 604–613** — `WebGLRenderer({antialias:true})`, `pixelRatio = min(devicePixelRatio, 2)`, `shadowMap.enabled=true` with `PCFSoftShadowMap`, sRGB output color space, `toneMapping = NoToneMapping`. No post-processing/EffectComposer anywhere in the file.
- **Camera rig — 620–637** — `OrthographicCamera`, `zoom=0.82`, fixed isometric pitch `ELEV = atan(1/√2)` (~35.26°), orbit radius `R=160`, half-height `VIEW=22`. `applyCamera()` recomputes frustum from aspect and repositions on a fixed elevation ring. Only azimuth changes.
- **Lighting rig — 639–657, 4413–4425** — `HemisphereLight` (sky `0xbcdcff`/ground `0x4e5a3c`) + a shadow-casting `DirectionalLight` "sun" (`0xffedd0`, pos `48,78,34`) + a non-shadow cool `fillLight` + a faint warm up-`bounce` light. Static positions; only colors/intensities animate at night. Shadow: `mapSize 2048²`, ortho shadow camera sized to the whole map, `bias −0.0004`, `normalBias 0.03`, `radius 3.2`.
- **Ground texture — 659–669, 771–935, 942–951** — A single `2176×2176` canvas (`N*PX = 68*32`), one `MeshPhongMaterial({shininess:2})` plane. `drawTile()` hand-paints each 32×32 tile cell (grass mottle, grass blades, shorelines, roads with `roadMask` connectivity, zoning hatch, driveways). Dirty tiles are batched via a `dirtyGround` Set and flushed once per frame (`flushGround`).
- **Overlay canvas — 671–682, 954–993, 3104–3140** — A second `2176²` canvas plane floating at `y=0.02` with `MeshBasicMaterial({transparent})` for hover highlights, road/zone previews, and the data-view heatmaps (power/traffic/pollution/land/crime/fire/education/zoning), all drawn as flat rgba rects.
- **Building geometry (`buildGeo`) — 993–1043** — Buildings are described as arrays of primitive "parts" (`box`/`cyl`/`cone`/`pyr`/`sph`), each merged into ONE non-indexed `BufferGeometry` with a baked vertex-color attribute. Color is shaded per-vertex by height (`_sh = 0.82 + 0.20*t + up?0.06`) — a **fake gradient AO/top-light**, not real ambient occlusion. A heuristic detects "glass" parts (cool+bright+blue) and brightens them so windows pop.
- **Procedural building designers — 1046–1618** — `massing()` picks one of 9 plan silhouettes (slab, L, T, cruciform, ziggurat, courtyard, pinwheel, spire…); `roofCap()` adds one of 5 crowns; `faceWindows()` glues tiny 0.04-thick boxes as window strips. Per-type generators: `resParts`, `comParts`, `indParts` (with `emit` smokestack points), `mixParts`, `officeParts`, `farmParts`, `plantParts` (coal/gas/solar/wind/nuclear), civic (`policeParts`/`fireParts`/`clinicParts`/`schoolParts`/`highParts`/`collegeParts`), `parkBuilt`, `protectParts`, `makeTreeMesh`.
- **setTileBuilding / construction anim — 1637–1661, 4563–4569** — Builds geometry, assigns shared `buildingMat`, faces the building toward an adjacent road (`ry = atan2`), then animates `scale.y` from 0.01→1 over 0.45s with `easeOutBack` (springy pop-up). This is the ONLY construction animation — no cranes/scaffold/foundation stages.
- **Vehicles — 1798–2030** — Shared `carGeo` (two boxes) with per-car `MeshLambertMaterial` random color; fire truck & police cruiser use vertex-colored `buildingMat`. Cars do graph-walk along road tiles toward weighted destinations (`pickDest`), with lane offset (`ox/oz = ±0.30`). Counts scale with population (up to ~320). Fire trucks dispatch to fires and can go off-road.
- **Smoke — 1734–1796** — Pool of 60 radial-gradient sprites; emitters come from building `emit` points (smokestacks). Also `roadPoof()` dust burst on road placement.
- **Clouds — 2069–2089** — 7 `Group`s of squashed white Lambert spheres drifting on `+x`, wrapping around; they cast shadows that sweep the map. Opacity fades with night.
- **City lights — 2044–2067, 4571–4580** — At dusk, `rebuildCityLights()` builds ONE `THREE.Points` cloud with additive blending: warm points for res/street lamps, cool for com/office, count scaling with level/density. Opacity driven by `nightT`.
- **Zap & flame sprites — 1663–1732** — Canvas-drawn sprite icons (no-power bolt; layered bézier flame) that pulse via `Math.sin` scale in the frame loop (4611–4618).
- **Roundabouts — 1823–1849** — Real 3D `Group`: `RingGeometry` asphalt + `CylinderGeometry` grass island + a shrub sphere, using shared Lambert mats.
- **Day/night — 4412–4431, 4598–4603** — Binary `nightTgt` 0/1; frame loop lerps `nightT` at rate 1.6/s and calls `applyNight()`, which lerps background/fog/hemi/sun colors and sets `buildingMat.emissive` for the warm window glow. Sun position never changes.
- **Frame loop — 4552–4622** — rAF; smooths azimuth, pans, steps construction anims, sim ticks, night lerp, cars, smoke, wind rotors, zaps/flames, then `flushGround()` + `renderer.render`.
- **Camera controls — 3202–3368** — Pointer pan (grab-world), pinch-zoom, **wheel zoom-to-cursor** (3314–3328, re-picks world point and re-centers), Q/E 90° rotation snaps, WASD/arrow keyboard pan. Zoom clamped `0.45–4.2`.

## NUMBERS (renderer settings, resolutions, mesh counts, palette values)

- Grid `N=68`, tile world size `T=2`, ground texels per tile `PX=32` → ground/overlay canvases are **2176×2176 px** each (≈32px/tile — quite low res; a building tile gets only 32×32 texels of ground under it).
- Renderer: `antialias:true`, `pixelRatio ≤ 2`, shadow map **2048×2048**, PCF-soft, `radius 3.2`, `bias −0.0004`, `normalBias 0.03`. `NoToneMapping`. sRGB output.
- Camera: ortho, `VIEW=22`, `zoom` default 0.82 (reset to 1.05 on load), pitch 35.26°, orbit R=160, zoom clamp 0.45–4.2, 4 azimuth snaps only.
- Materials: **all 3D uses `MeshLambertMaterial`** (buildingMat `flatShading:true, vertexColors:true`; treeMat vertexColors; roundabout/cloud/car Lambert). Ground is `MeshPhongMaterial{shininess:2}`. Overlay/sprite = `MeshBasicMaterial`/`SpriteMaterial`. **No `MeshStandardMaterial`/PBR anywhere.**
- Vertex-shade AO formula: `_sh = 0.82 + 0.20*heightT + (faceUp?0.06:0)`; glass parts multiplied ~1.12–1.16 + offset.
- Lights: sun `0xffedd0` int 1.15 (night lerps 0.95→0.16); hemi 0.44/0.85→0.45; fill `0xa8c4ee` 0.26; bounce `0xffe8c0` 0.14.
- Life systems: smoke pool **60** sprites; **7** clouds (3–5 spheres each); **3** wind turbines/rotors; traffic cars up to **~320** (scales with pop), police ≤6, fire ≤20; city lights = single Points cloud.
- Scene colors: bg day `0x6bb0e6`, fog `0x9ec8e8` (range `N*T*3.4 … N*T*6.5`), rim base `0x2e3f32`. Day/night sets `DAYC`/`NITC` (bg `0xa9d2ec`→`0x101a2e`, sun `0xfff2d8`→`0x9db8ff`, emissive `0x000000`→`0x553d12`).
- Building palettes: `WALL_RES` 8 saturated mids (`#f0a860`,`#ff9a5c`,`#f5c245`,`#7cc86a`,`#5bb0d8`,`#ef7d92`,`#b98ad8`,`#ffb347`); `ROOF_RES` 6 (`#e0492e`…`#2f7d9a`); `ACCENTS` 6; office glass palette 6 vivid (`#18c4b4`,`#1aa8e8`,`#3a7cf0`,`#20c070`,`#7a5cf0`,`#f07020`). Water fill `#39689c`/`#4178ad` with 2 static sparkle dots/tile.
- Construction anim: 0.45s, `easeOutBack`.

## WEAKNESSES (what caps the visual quality today)

1. **Flat world, no elevation.** `genTerrain` (710–769) only tags tiles grass/water; the ground is one flat plane. No hills, valleys, coast bluffs, or terraced building sites — the single biggest limiter on "wow."
2. **One matte material for everything.** `MeshLambertMaterial` + vertex color means no specular highlights, no glass reflectivity, no metal, no roughness variation, no normal/texture maps on buildings. Glass towers are just brighter flat blue boxes (the `_isGlass` hack, 1020–1032).
3. **Fake AO only.** The height-gradient shade (1028–1033) is a cheap vertical gradient, not contact/ambient occlusion. Buildings don't ground themselves — no darkening where a wall meets the ground or where masses meet, so objects look "placed on top of" the plane rather than sitting in it.
4. **Static sun / binary night.** The `DirectionalLight` never moves (642); "night" is a 2-state color lerp (4426). No dawn/dusk gradient, no long raking shadows, no golden hour — shadows always fall the same direction.
5. **No real water.** Water is painted pixels in the ground canvas (786–800) with 2 static sparkles; there's no water mesh, no animated normals, no reflection/refraction, no shoreline foam beyond a 5px sand strip.
6. **Low ground resolution.** 32 texels/tile makes roads, shorelines, and lot detail blocky up close; the whole ground is one 2176² texture that must be re-uploaded (`gTex.needsUpdate`) on any tile change.
7. **No tone mapping / no bloom / no post FX.** `NoToneMapping` and no EffectComposer means night city lights, window glow, and smokestacks can't bloom; there's no SSAO, no DOF, no color grading, no FXAA/TAA beyond MSAA.
8. **Sprites read as flat billboards.** Smoke, flames, zap icons, and city lights are camera-facing 2D — fine at this scale but they break the miniature illusion when the camera rotates.
9. **Rotation is 4-snap only.** No free orbit, no pitch control — the diorama can only be viewed from 4 fixed corners.
10. **No weather/seasons, no pedestrians, no traffic realism** (cars pass through each other, no stopping at intersections, no signals), and construction is a single scale-up pop with no intermediate states.

## OPPORTUNITIES (highest-leverage upgrades, each with concrete technique and hook point)

1. **Switch to PBR + tone mapping (biggest visual ROI).** Replace `buildingMat`/`treeMat` (988–989) with `MeshStandardMaterial({vertexColors, flatShading})`, add per-part `roughness`/`metalness` via a second vertex attribute (glass low-rough/metal, concrete high-rough), set `renderer.toneMapping = ACESFilmicToneMapping` + `toneMappingExposure` (611), and add a small procedural `PMREMGenerator` environment map (or a gradient `RoomEnvironment`) so glass towers actually reflect the sky. This alone moves the look from "flat toy" to "rendered." Hook: material defs at 988, renderer at 604–611, buildGeo attribute at 1024–1035.
2. **Bake real vertex AO into `buildGeo`.** In the per-part vertex loop (1024–1035), darken vertices near the ground (`y→0`) and near concave mass junctions, and add a soft contact-shadow decal (a radial-gradient sprite/plane) under each building in `setTileBuilding` (1637). Cheap, and it "sits" buildings into the world. Alternatively add SSAO via `EffectComposer` + `SAOPass` for global AO.
3. **Continuous time-of-day sun cycle.** Replace the binary `toggleNight`/`applyNight` (4416–4431) with a normalized `timeOfDay` [0,1) that drives `sun.position` around an arc in the frame loop, interpolates sky/sun/fog colors through a dawn→noon→dusk→night gradient (extend `DAYC`/`NITC` into a keyframed ramp at 4414), and ramps `cityLights.opacity` + `buildingMat.emissive` on a curve. Long raking shadows at golden hour are the single most "cinematic" cheap win.
4. **Real water surface.** Add a translucent water `PlaneGeometry` mesh at the lake tiles (or one map-wide plane masked to water tiles) with a custom `ShaderMaterial` or scrolling normal map for animated ripples, plus a simple sky reflection/Fresnel tint. Hook: create alongside `ground` (666–682); drive water tiles from `genTerrain`'s `terrain==='water'` set (723–737).
5. **Add gentle elevation / heightfield terrain.** Give `genTerrain` (710) a `vnoise`-based height per tile, displace the ground plane vertices (subdivide the `PlaneGeometry`), and offset building/tree/road `y` by tile height in `setTileBuilding` (1645) and `tileCX/tileCZ`. More invasive (sim/pathing assume flat) but highest ceiling.
6. **Weather & seasons.** Particle rain/snow (reuse sprite-pool pattern from smoke, 1734) and a seasonal palette lerp on grass (`mixRGB` in `drawTile`, 804) and tree colors (`makeTreeMesh`, 1500) — autumn oranges, winter snow on ground canvas + roofs. Ties to existing month clock (`S.month`).
7. **Pedestrians & richer traffic.** Reuse the car graph-walker (`updateCars`, 1949) for tiny people via `InstancedMesh` on sidewalks near developed tiles; intersection slowdown/stop logic and simple signals at `roadMask` junctions (911).
8. **Multi-stage construction.** Extend `constructionAnims` (597, 4563): foundation/scaffold box first, rise floor-by-floor, crane primitive removed on completion. Hook: `setTileBuilding` (1655).
9. **Bloom & emissive night glow.** `EffectComposer` + `UnrealBloomPass` so `cityLights` (2044), window emissive (4424), streetlamps, smokestack embers, police beacons glow at night.
10. **Camera polish.** Free orbit + pitch with smooth interpolation (currently 4-snap, 3345); keep existing zoom-to-cursor (3314–3328). Tighter shadow frustum following `camTarget` (651–654) when zoomed.
11. **Higher-fidelity ground.** Bump `PX` from 32 (513) toward 64, or tile/atlas the ground texture so up-close roads/shorelines aren't blocky.
